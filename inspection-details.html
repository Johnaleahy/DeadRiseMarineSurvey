Marine Survey Inspection Details

Our comprehensive marine surveys cover all critical areas of your vessel. Below is a detailed breakdown of what we inspect during each survey type.

Hull & Structure

  • Hull integrity and condition
  • Transom inspection
  • Keel and rudder assessment
  • Through-hull fittings
  • Deck structure and hardware
  • Bulkheads and frames
  • Stanchions and lifelines
  • Hatches and ports
  • Gelcoat and paint condition
  • Core sampling (if applicable)

Mechanical Systems

  • Engine condition and operation
  • Propeller and shaft inspection
  • Cooling system evaluation
  • Fuel system and tanks
  • Exhaust system
  • Engine mounts and alignment
  • Transmission and controls
  • Steering system
  • Trim tabs and controls
  • Windlass and anchor system

Electrical Systems

  • Main electrical panel
  • Battery condition and installation
  • Shore power connections
  • Charging systems
  • Navigation lights
  • Interior lighting
  • Bilge pump wiring
  • Electronics installation
  • Bonding system
  • ABYC compliance check

Plumbing Systems

  • Fresh water system
  • Waste water system
  • Bilge pump operation
  • Seacocks and through-hulls
  • Hoses and fittings
  • Hot water heater
  • Head and sanitation system
  • Shower and drain systems
  • Pressure water system
  • Thru-hull valve operation

Safety Equipment

  • Fire extinguishers
  • Life jackets and PFDs
  • Flares and signaling devices
  • First aid kit
  • Emergency radio equipment
  • Horn and sound signals
  • Navigation equipment
  • Safety harnesses and tethers
  • Emergency steering
  • Man overboard equipment

Interior & Accommodations

  • Cabin ventilation
  • Interior structural integrity
  • Galley equipment
  • Refrigeration systems
  • Cabinetry and joinerwork
  • Upholstery condition
  • Windows and ports
  • Interior lighting
  • Heating and air conditioning
  • Storage areas and lockers

Rigging & Sails (Sailboats)

  • Standing rigging inspection
  • Running rigging condition
  • Mast and boom integrity
  • Sail condition assessment
  • Winches and hardware
  • Furling systems
  • Blocks and cleats
  • Rigging attachment points
  • Mast step and support
  • Spreaders and shrouds

Documentation Review

  • Title and registration
  • Previous survey reports
  • Maintenance records
  • Warranty documentation
  • Insurance requirements
  • Coast Guard documentation
  • Engine service records
  • Equipment manuals
  • Modification history
  • Safety equipment certificates
